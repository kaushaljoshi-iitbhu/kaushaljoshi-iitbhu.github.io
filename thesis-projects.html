Master’s Thesis

Entanglement Studies in the AKLT Spin-1 Biquadratic Model

This work focuses on numerical studies of quantum entanglement in the Affleck–Kennedy–Lieb–Tasaki (AKLT) spin-1 model. Entanglement measures such as Wootters’ concurrence and von Neumann entropy were employed to analyze bipartite correlations. The ground state properties and indications of phase transitions were investigated using numerical techniques, including optimized matrix representations and parallel computation.

Institution: VNIT Nagpur

Supervisor: Dr. M. S. Ramkarthik

Research Projects

Quantum Entanglement in Multi-Qubit Systems

Summer Research Fellowship at IIT Dharwad focused on the covariance matrix criterion for detecting entanglement in multi-qubit systems. The work explored extensions to higher qubit numbers and limitations in quantifying entanglement measures.

Radio Astronomy & RGBC Image Analysis

Analysis of extragalactic radio sources using multi-wavelength RGBC imaging under the RAD@Home program. The project involved FITS file analysis and interpretation of astrophysical structures.

Positional Astronomy at Jantar Mantar

Practical observational project focused on positional astronomy using historical instruments at Jantar Mantar, New Delhi. Calculated solar altitude, azimuth, and local time for different locations.
