Master’s Thesis

Entanglement Studies in the AKLT Spin-1 Biquadratic Model

This thesis presents a numerical investigation of quantum entanglement in the Affleck–Kennedy–Lieb–Tasaki (AKLT) spin-1 biquadratic model. Entanglement measures such as Wootters’ concurrence and von Neumann entropy were employed to analyze bipartite correlations.

Institution: VNIT Nagpur

Supervisor: Dr. M. S. Ramkarthik

Research Projects

Quantum Entanglement in Multi-Qubit Systems

Summer Research Fellowship project focused on detecting quantum entanglement in multi-qubit systems using the covariance matrix criterion. The work involved extending the criterion to higher-dimensional systems and understanding its limitations in quantifying entanglement.

Institution: Indian Institute of Technology, Dharwad, Karnataka

Supervisor: Dr. R. Prabhu

Radio Astronomy and RGBC Image Analysis

Research work under the RAD@Home program involving the analysis of extragalactic radio sources through RGBC image construction. Multi-wavelength FITS data were analyzed to study morphological features and identify unusual radio galaxy structures.

Institution: RAD@Home

Supervisor: Dr. Ananda Hota

Positional Astronomy at Jantar Mantar

Observational project centered on historical astronomical instruments at Jantar Mantar, New Delhi. The work included calculating the Sun’s altitude and azimuth, determining local time for multiple locations, and understanding the working principles of traditional Indian astronomical instruments.

Institution: Nehru Memorial Museum & Library, New Delhi

Supervisor: Mrs. Megha Rajoria
